Our Mission

Campus Share was founded on a simple idea: access is better than ownership. We are the first peer-to-peer "Library of Things" exclusively for the KNUST community. We connect students who have things with students who need them, creating a sustainable and affordable ecosystem right here on campus. We don't own any items; we simply provide the secure platform that makes sharing possible.

How to Rent an Item

  1. 1 Browse the All Rentals page and find the item you need.
  2. 2 Click the "Rent" button to open a WhatsApp chat with us. The item name will be pre-filled.
  3. 3 We will confirm the item's availability and guide you through a one-time security verification (for first-time renters).
  4. 4 Pay the rental fee and a small, refundable security deposit via MoMo.
  5. 5 We connect you with the item's owner for pickup. Once returned safely, your deposit is refunded instantly.

Security & Accountability Protocol

Trust is the foundation of our community. To protect item owners and ensure accountability, we have a mandatory verification process and clear terms of use.

First-Time Renter Verification

To rent for the first time, you will be required to provide the following on WhatsApp:

  • A clear, current selfie.
  • A clear photo of the FRONT of your valid Ghana Card.
  • A clear photo of the BACK of your valid Ghana Card.
  • And a clear photo of your student ID
This information is stored securely for verification purposes only and is never shared publicly.

Accountability for Damages

By using our service to rent an item, you explicitly agree that you are fully responsible for returning it in the same condition you received it. If an item is damaged, lost, or stolen while in your care, you will be held accountable and will be required to pay for the repair or full replacement cost. Failure to do so will result in being banned from the platform and further action.

User Agreement & Theft Policy

By using this service, you confirm that you have provided your valid Ghana Card and Student ID. You understand that if an item is not returned by the agreed-upon time without communication, Campus Share has the right to use these documents to file a formal report with the KNUST University Police for theft of property.
